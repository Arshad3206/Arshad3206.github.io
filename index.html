Research Portfolio

1. Exit Profile Effects in Square Supersonic Nozzles

Investigating how exit-profile design influences the acoustic and flow behavior of square supersonic nozzles. This work compares conventional square nozzle configurations with designs incorporating convergent-divergent contouring on both sides of the nozzle to evaluate whether square nozzles can approach the acoustic behavior of circular nozzles while preserving geometric advantages for future thrust-vectoring applications.

2. Upstream Geometry Effects on Twin Supersonic Jets

Studying how upstream chamber geometry affects twin supersonic jet acoustics even when the nominal nozzle design conditions remain the same. This project highlights how facility-specific implementation details can strongly influence screech feedback mechanisms, multimode behavior, and repeatability across different experimental studies.

3. Throat Location Effects on Circular Jet Acoustics

Investigating the influence of throat location on the acoustic behavior of circular supersonic nozzles. This work examines an underexplored nozzle design parameter and evaluates how changes in throat placement affect jet development, shock-cell structure, and acoustic radiation.

4. Fluidic Injection and Trapezoidal Nozzle Studies

Assisted in experimental studies of fluidic injection and trapezoidal nozzle configurations for supersonic jet noise reduction. These investigations focus on how injection improves flow mixing, modifies turbulent structures, and affects the acoustic behavior of non-axisymmetric supersonic jets.

5. Flow Visualization and Acoustic Analysis Methods

Applying advanced experimental and data-analysis methods to understand turbulent flow structures and acoustic radiation in supersonic jets. Methods include Schlieren visualization, SPOD, spectrogram analysis, wave-number analysis, and modal processing techniques such as MPT.
