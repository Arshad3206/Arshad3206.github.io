About Me

I am an aerospace engineering PhD researcher at the University of Cincinnati, working on supersonic jet acoustics, nozzle flow physics, and jet noise reduction. My research focuses on the design and analysis of nozzles for jet noise mitigation, with experimental work involving far-field acoustics, Schlieren visualization, and Particle Image Velocimetry (PIV).

My broader technical background includes experimental fluid dynamics, aeroacoustics, data analysis, model validation, simulation workflows, and engineering design. I have experience with MATLAB, Python, Dymola, ANSYS Fluent, Flow3D, COMSOL, SolidWorks, CATIA, AutoCAD, and NX Siemens.

Research Interests

  • Jet Noise Reduction
  • Nozzle Design
  • Fluidic Injection
  • Schlieren Imaging
  • Particle Image Velocimetry (PIV)
  • Experimental Aeroacoustics
  • CFD and Model Validation

Current Focus

My current work investigates the acoustic and flow-field behavior of supersonic jets, including rectangular nozzles, twin nozzle configurations, screech feedback mechanisms, broadband shock-associated noise, and fluidic injection techniques for jet noise control.
